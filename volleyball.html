VOLLEY BALL

No Upcoming Events!

Number of players

  • Six players per team on the court at any given time, with substitutions allowed.
  • Roster size usually includes more players, depending on league or tournament rules.

    • Strategy of game

  • Offense: Aim to score points by grounding the ball on the opponent's court through various attacks (spikes, tips, etc.)
  • Defense: Prevent the opponent from scoring by blocking their attacks, digging them, and keeping the ball in play for your team to counter-attack.
  • Rotation: Players rotate positions clockwise after each point, requiring versatility and teamwork.
  • Communication: Essential for coordinating team movements, calling plays, and anticipating opponent's actions.

    • Is it a team game?

    Absolutely! While individual skill plays a role, success heavily relies on coordinated plays, communication, and teamwork. Each player needs to understand their position, support teammates, and adjust based on the game situation.

    Rules of the game

  • Each team gets three touches to return the ball over the net before it must be sent back to the opponent's side.
  • Points are scored when the ball lands on the opponent's court within the lines, or when the opponent commits a fault (double hit, foot fault, etc.).
  • Sets are played to 25 points with a two-point lead required to win (except for the fifth set, which goes to 15 points).
  • Matches typically consist of best-of-three or best-of-five sets.

    • Some important facts about the game

  • Invented in the United States in 1895.
  • Now a popular sport worldwide, played at recreational and professional levels.
  • Olympic sport since 1964 (beach volleyball added in 1996).
  • Requires athleticism, agility, teamwork, and quick thinking.
  • Emphasizes fair play and sportsmanship.

    • Statistics/ Records

  • Highest team score in an ODI: England (498) vs Netherlands (2023).
  • Most Olympic medals: Brazil (11 gold, 3 silver, 2 bronze).
  • Most career kills: Milagros Cabello (Peru) - 7,968.
  • Most career digs: Kim Yeon-koung (South Korea) - 2,705.
