BADMINTON

No Upcoming Events!

Number of players

  • Singles: 1 player on each side of the court.
  • Doubles: 2 players on each side of the court, forming a team.

    • Strategy of game

  • Badminton involves fast-paced rallies and requires agility, reflexes, and endurance.
  • Players use various strategies, such as offensive smashes, defensive clears, drops, and net shots.
  • Positioning, footwork, and communication (in doubles) are crucial for success.

    • Is it a team game?

  • Singles is individual, while doubles can be considered a team game within itself.
  • Badminton can also be played in team competitions with multiple singles and doubles matches across different categories.

    • Rules of the game

  • The game is played indoors on a rectangular court divided by a net.
  • Points are scored when the shuttlecock lands within the opponent's court.
  • Each side can only hit the shuttlecock once before it passes over the net.
  • The server must serve diagonally across the net, and points are scored on the opponent's serve.
  • Points: Best of 3 games, up to 21 points (with a 2-point lead at the end).
  • Serving: Underarm, diagonally across the court.
  • Faults: Hitting the net, shuttlecock landing out of bounds, exceeding one hit per side.
  • Doubles: Players have designated serving positions and specific serving order.

    • Some important facts about the game

  • Invented in England, badminton became an Olympic sport in 1992.
  • It's popular worldwide, especially in Asia.
  • Requires agility, stamina, and quick reflexes.
  • Considered a gentle sport despite its fast pace.
  • The shuttlecock, also known as a birdie, is made of feathers or synthetic materials.
  • Badminton is known for its fast shuttle speeds, with top players hitting smashes exceeding 200 mph.

    • Statistics/ Records

  • Fastest Smash: Lin Dan (CHN) - 493 km/h.
  • Most Olympic Medals: Denmark - 21 (Gold, Silver, Bronze).
  • Most Men's Singles World Championships: Lin Dan (CHN) - 5 Titles.
  • Most Women's Singles World Championships: Zhang Ning (CHN) - 2 Titles.
  • Fastest Smash: Some of the fastest recorded smashes have exceeded 200 mph.
  • Longest Rally: The longest rally in badminton history lasted for over 100 minutes.
