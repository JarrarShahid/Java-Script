Number of players

  • 22 players in total.
  • 11 players from each team.

    • Strategy of game

  • Offense: Aims to score goals by passing, dribbling, and controlling the ball, creating scoring opportunities through individual skill and coordinated team movements. Different formations and tactics are used depending on the team's strengths and weaknesses.
  • Defense: Prevents the opponent from scoring by positioning, tackling, and intercepting passes. Teamwork and communication are crucial in maintaining a strong defensive shape.

    • Is it a team game?

    Absolutely! While individual skill plays a role, success heavily relies on teamwork. Players need to coordinate movements, communicate effectively, and understand their individual roles within the team's strategy. Formations, set pieces, and counter-attacks all require seamless teamwork to be successful.

    Rules of the game

  • 11 players per team on the field at any time.
  • Two halves of 45 minutes each, with extra time and penalties used to break ties in knockout matches , with added injury or stoppage time.
  • Players use their feet, head, and chest to control the ball, with hands only allowed for the goalkeeper within their penalty area.
  • Goals are scored by kicking the ball into the opponent's net.
  • Offenses include fouls, yellow cards, and red cards, leading to free kicks, penalties, or player dismissals.
  • A match is officiated by referees and assistant referees.

    • Some important facts about the game

  • Football is the world's most popular sport, played and followed by millions of people globally.
  • The Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA) is the international governing body for football.
  • The FIFA World Cup is one of the most-watched and prestigious football tournaments, held every four years.
  • Requires athleticism, skill, tactical understanding, and teamwork

    • Statistics/ Records

  • Goals: Cristiano Ronaldo (Portugal) - most international goals (118).
  • Assists: Lionel Messi (Argentina) - most career assists (351).
  • Most World Cup titles: Brazil (5).
  • Most goals in a single World Cup: Just Fontaine (France) - 13 goals (1958).
  • Fastest goal: Miroslav Stoch (Slovakia) - 8.35 seconds (2012).
