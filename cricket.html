    • Strategy of game

    Each team takes turns batting and bowling/fielding. The batting team aims to score as many runs as possible by hitting the ball and running between the wickets, while the bowling/fielding team tries to dismiss the batsmen and prevent runs. Different formats of cricket emphasize different strategies, with shorter formats favoring aggressive batting and bowling, and longer formats requiring patience and resilience.

    Is it a team game?

    Yes, cricket is a team sport. Although individual brilliance plays a role, winning relies on coordination, communication, and collective effort across all batsmen, bowlers, and fielders.

    Rules of the game

  • The game is played on an oval field with a rectangular pitch at the center.
  • Each team gets two innings.
  • An over consists of six deliveries bowled by one bowler.
  • Batsmen score runs by hitting the ball and running between the wickets or hitting the ball to the boundary.
  • Batsmen are dismissed in multiple ways, including being bowled, caught, run out, or LBW (Leg Before Wicket).
  • Different formats have variations in rules, like the number of overs and innings, and fielding restrictions.
  • Various formats have different overs per innings (e.g., Test matches have unlimited overs per innings, ODIs have 50 overs per innings, and T20Is have 20 overs per innings).
  • The team scoring the most runs wins the match.

    • Some important facts about the game

  • Cricket originated in England in the 16th century and is now popular worldwide, particularly in Commonwealth countries.
  • Cricket has three main formats: Test matches, One Day Internationals (ODIs), and Twenty20 Internationals (T20Is).
  • Test cricket is the longest format, played over five days, while One Day Internationals (ODIs) last around 8 hours and Twenty20 matches are completed in about 3 hours.
  • Cricket is known as a gentlemen's game, emphasizing fair play and sportsmanship.
  • The Ashes is a historic Test series played between England and Australia.

    • Statistics/ Records

  • Sachin Tendulkar (India) holds the record for most runs scored in Test cricket (34,357) and ODIs (18,426).
  • Shane Warne (Australia) has the most Test wickets (708) and Muttiah Muralitharan (Sri Lanka) has the most ODI wickets (534).
  • The highest team score in an ODI is 498 by England against Netherlands in 2023.
